10 Techniques for overcoming low Self-esteem

Live in the moment

You can choose your decisions consciously and wisely when you are concentrating on the present
moment, untouched by past hurts and unconcerned about fears or expectations for the future.

Develop awareness

We should recognize how we are responding and reacting to our own fears when we are conscious,
creating a gap between our feelings and our behaviors. Then we have the option of responding in a
healthier manner.

Write in a journal

Many of our thoughts and emotions are stored in our subconscious mind, and writing will assist us in
bringing them to light. Writing about our feelings and thoughts will help us distinguish between our
negative self-perceptions and the reality of who we are.

Be non-judgemental

When we approach our lives without judgment, we simply embrace ourselves, our experiences, our
mistakes and achievements, and other people for who they are, no matter how good or poor, proud
or ashamed they may be.

Stay connected to yourself

By helping you to avoid the autopilot thought and behavior that keeps you jumping to satisfy others
without considering your own needs, mindfulness will help you build a sense of connection to
yourself and reduce your people-pleasing ways.


     

Practice mindful meditation

Meditation simply entails letting go of your racing thoughts and realizing that your thoughts, feelings,
and values are fleeting rather than permanent. Every day, take a few moments to simply be still,
concentrate on your breathing, and watch your worries dissipate like clouds.

Participate in your own life

Mindfulness helps us to take control of our lives by encouraging us to be involved and assertive.
You will take action and engage in your own life by being aware of your feelings and choosing your
answers to them.

Develop a beginner’s mind

When you have a beginner's mind, you approach things with openness, eagerness, and a lack of
expectation, as if you're seeing them for the first time. Instead of immediately answering with the
same old patterns of behavior, you can see things in a new light.

Let go

The purpose of mindfulness is non-attachment, or letting go. You can trust yourself to do what is
best for you when you let go of what you think you should do or who you should be.

Show compassion toward yourself

You, like anyone else, are deserving of love. Self-compassion simply entails giving yourself the
affection, security, and acceptance that you need.
