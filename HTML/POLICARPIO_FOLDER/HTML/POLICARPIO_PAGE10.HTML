How To Love Yourself



5 Actual, Real Ways to Practice Self Love



How to Love Yourself: 10 Simple Ideas

  1. Know who you are. If you don't know who you are, it's hard to love yourself. Invest time in figuring
    out what you believe, trust, and enjoy. Here's where you can learn more about getting to know
    yourself.

  2. When you need to, say "no." Boundaries are an important part of self-care because they
    communicate to others that you want and deserve respect. More information on establishing
    boundaries can be found here.

  3. Don't measure yourself against others. Others aren't better or worse than you, nor are they more
    or less; they're just different. You are valuable in your own right, and respecting yourself eliminates
    the need for comparisons. Learn more on how to stop making comparisons.

  4. Be fully present in the moment. Distractions abound in our lives. While many of these activities are
    enjoyable and worthwhile, they can also be draining and prevent us from fully understanding and
    being ourselves. More information on mindfulness can be found here.

  5. Recognize and play to your strengths. We all have incredible abilities, but many of them go
    unappreciated. It's difficult to access these wonderful qualities when you're busy and distracted.
    Focusing on your strengths will help you feel better about yourself.

  6. Allow yourself to be pampered. A treat is a special gift that you offer to yourself. It does not have to
    be paid, unlike a reward. Giving yourself treats "only because" is a great way to be kind to yourself.

  7. Be truthful about yourself. This one could be more difficult than it seems. Some of us are so skilled
    at deceit that we aren't even aware of it. In all relationships, honesty is essential, and your
    relationship with yourself is no exception. If you're misleading, minimizing, or making excuses, it's
    clear that you can't love your whole messy self. True self-love entails accepting responsibility and
    accountability for one's actions.

  8. Allow yourself to be forgiven for your flaws and errors. You put a lot of pressure on yourself. You're
    probably the most critical of yourself. Allow yourself to relax and accept your humanity. It's normal to
    make mistakes. Imperfections are an essential part of who you are. More information on
    self-forgiveness can be found here.

  9. Work on forgiving yourself when it comes to the bigger issues. We often bring larger regrets or
    transgressions with us. Self-forgiveness is a gradual method of accepting that you did your best.
    Of course, you will do better today. It's true that hindsight is 20/20, which is why judging your past
    selves with the experience you have now is totally unreasonable. “When we know better, we do
    better,” as the saying goes.

  10. Accept the fact that other people would dislike you. That's right, some people dislike you, and that's
    perfectly fine. Don't waste your time attempting to please people who are difficult to please or who
    aren't particularly important to you. Being yourself entails letting go of your people-pleasing habits
    and embracing your true self. Learn more about satisfying others.
