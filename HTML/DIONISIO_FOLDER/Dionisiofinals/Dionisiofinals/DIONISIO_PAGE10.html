LOVE YOURSELF WITH 3 AMAZING SELF-LOVE TIPS FROM BTS


True Love First Begins With Loving Myself.

There's an old adage that says you can't love someone else unless you love yourself (which we've all probably heard a million times). Although this isn't entirely true, how we feel about ourselves has a significant influence on the relationships we develop and how we perceive them. We appear to be less happy with our relationships and less hopeful about the future when we have low self-esteem. We may also underestimate how much our partners love us because of our lack of self-love. When we don't feel good about ourselves, it's difficult to imagine that anyone else would. Worse, a lack of self-love often contributes to more negative relationships... it makes us more likely to choose and remain with partners who do not treat us well.

People seek self-verification, according to Dr. Bill Swann, a Professor of Social and Personality Psychology at the University of Texas at Austin. Others should be able to recognize and understand us. But it is from inside that this experience and understanding emerges. Dr. Swann's experiments and Self-Verification Theory suggest that if we have a strong conviction about ourselves, we want to be surrounded by people who share that belief. Even though they are unfavorable values. A low sense of self-worth can make us feel attracted to people who don't respect us as much. It can make us feel as if we don't deserve happiness or a good result.

What conclusions can we draw from all of this?
It is true that love begins with you. We are all deserving of self-acceptance and affection. And it is important that we focus on this. Finally, learning to appreciate yourself helps you to see that you, too, are deserving of others' respect. You're more likely to make supportive and caring relationships a priority. You'll get a more favorable impression of them. And you won't force yourself to stay in a bad situation because you'll realize you really deserve better.


Maybe I Made A Mistake Yesterday, But Yesterday’s Me Is Still Me. Today, I Am Who I Am With All Of My Faults And My Mistakes. Tomorrow, I Might Be A Tiny Bit Wiser, And That Will Be Me Too.

Self-love, in my opinion, is a combination of self-esteem, self-compassion, and self-acceptance. Yeah, it's about appreciating your worth and adoring your individuality. However, it also entails admitting your flaws, knowing your shortcomings, and completely embracing everything. As R.M. demonstrated in this lovely quote from his BTS Love Yourself United Nations Speech.

Why would you love and accept a fault, weakness or mistake?
And it's all added up to you! And aren't you wonderful just the way you are? Yes, I believe so! You are the sum of your interactions, according to B.J. Neblett. Positive, negative, simple, or difficult... they've all contributed to who you are today. Clinical psychologist Leon F. Seltzer, Ph.D., author of Paradoxical Strategies of Psychotherapy, has this to say about embracing ourselves, flaws and all:

You need to recognize that given your defenses, blind spots, insecurities, and the harmful things you may have been exposed to, you really are doing the best you can—and have been all along. Additionally, you need to stop evaluating yourself according to standards that don’t really fit who you are, or what you can realistically expect of yourself.

It's such an important step to realize that you don't have to be some imaginary better version of yourself to be deserving of love. Perfection is impossible to achieve, and it has no place in our self-love path. There will never be a perfect version of you. There isn't a single version of someone that is without flaws. That's fine! We are all deserving just as we are, faults and all, right now.


I Have Come To Love Myself For Who I Am, For Who I Was, And For Who I Hope To Become.

This lovely thinking encourages us to respect ourselves for who we were and who we are, flaws and mistakes included. It also serves as a helpful reminder that self-love isn't about being complacent. It's not wrong to love and support yourself as you are right now. It's also not wrong to have dreams, ambitions, or expectations for self-improvement. You should have goals while also appreciating yourself for who you are. This quote from R.M's BTS Love Yourself UN Speech is part of the point. You are free to aspire, dream, and grow. As long as you don't deprive yourself of self-love until you've achieved those goals. We all want to learn and develop, and we all deserve it. But we can't keep our self-love hostage until we transform into some phantom "better" version of ourselves. You are all deserving of self-love, no matter who you were, who you are, or who you would become.

You're just overflowing with goodness. Things begin to improve as you understand this and begin to honor the amazing person you already are. Because any improvement you seek is no longer about repairing a shattered and worthless self; it's about recognizing that you're already overflowing at the seams with goodness and simply looking to add to it. Life is difficult. You are deserving of your own affection. And you are entitled to your own defense. Regardless of where you are on your route.
