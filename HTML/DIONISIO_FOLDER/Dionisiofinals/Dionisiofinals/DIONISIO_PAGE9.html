BTS

For those unfamiliar with BTS, they are a seven-member Korean boy band who debuted in June of 2013. The group's acronym comes from its Korean name, Bangtan Sonyeondan (방탄소년단), which translates to "Bulletproof Boy Scouts." It is made up of three rappers (RM, SUGA, and j-hope) and four vocalists (Jin, Jimin, V, and Jungkook).

The name was chosen to reflect the group's main goal: to protect and speak about the issues and concerns of young people in their twenties. Their first single, "No More Dream," addressed the issue of children squandering their futures and aspirations in the name of mindless learning. The "School Trilogy," which consisted of their first three songs, continued on this theme while also criticizing other facets of society. BTS's music has always been about youth and social commentary. The Love Yourself series, on the other hand, has taken the community beyond simply criticizing society and is an effort to provide some solutions.

The group started working on the Love Yourself series in March of 2016, and the first album was released in September of 2017. This was before their second full album, Wings, catapulted them to the top of the K-pop scene, and long before they gained mainstream exposure in the west. It would be over a year before they were nominated for Top Social Artist at the Billboard Music Awards.

BTS had moderate success in 2015 after the release of their two-part film, "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life (화양연화)." They had debuted from a small agency in relative obscurity and had achieved a moderate degree of success after the release of their two-part series, "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life (화양연화)." In early 2016, their star was rising, but they were still a long way from being the juggernaut they would become in the next two years. SUGA (the group's lead rapper and a regular producer) and RM both characterized 2015 and 2016 as mentally trying years for them. In August of 2016, SUGA released Agust D, a mixtape in which he openly discussed his experiences with depression and social anxiety. RM released “Always” at the end of the same year, a song that began with the verses, One morning, I opened my eyes And wished I was dead I hoped someone would kill me In this loud silence.

Despite their financial success, BTS was often the subject of internet attacks and criticism, including claims that their company had exaggerated their earnings (which were proven false after Big Hit Entertainment demanded an official investigation) and that they were guilty of "plagiarism" because they had appeared in school uniforms after another — The year before, a more famous — and at the time — boy group had worn school uniforms for their idea. So the Love Yourself series was born in this environment of unsure achievement, ongoing hardship, and constant criticism. RM once wrote on the group's fancafe (a kind of private message board for fans and members of the group to post on) that loving yourself can feel like an unattainable heat shimmer flickering before your eyes. The Love Yourself series is a result of the seven members' search for the heat shimmer and what they discovered along the way.
