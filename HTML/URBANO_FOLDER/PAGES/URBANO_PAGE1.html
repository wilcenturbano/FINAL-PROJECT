Psychological Aspect

Self-Awareness


            The true meaning of self awareness
        
(how to tell if you are actually aware)

Self-awareness is the ability to focus on yourself and how your actions, thoughts, or emotions do or don’t align with your internal standards. If you’re highly self-aware, you can objectively evaluate yourself, manage your emotions, align your behavior with your values ,and understand correctly how others perceive you.
Self-awareness involves being tuned in to totally different aspects of the self together with traits, behaviors, and feelings. primarily, it's a status during which oneself becomes the main focus of attention.      


Types of Self-Awareness

Psychologists often break self-awareness down into two different types, either public or private.

Public Self-Awareness

This type emerges once individuals area unit awake to however they seem to others. Public awareness usually emerges in things once individuals area unit at the middle of attention, like once giving a presentation or reproof a bunch of friends. This type of awarenesse usually compels individuals to stick to social norms. When we are aware that we are being watched and evaluated, we often try to behave in ways that are socially acceptable and desirable. Public awareness also can cause analysis anxiety within which individuals become distressed, anxious, or disturbed concerning however they're perceived by others.

Private Self-Awareness

Private Self-Awareness This type happens when people become aware of some aspects of themselves, but only in a private way. For example, seeing your face in the mirror is a type of private self-awareness.
