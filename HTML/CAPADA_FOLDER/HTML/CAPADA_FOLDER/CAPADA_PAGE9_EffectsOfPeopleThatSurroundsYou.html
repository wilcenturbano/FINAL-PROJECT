Effects Of People That Surrounds You

>

We are more likely to become like strong, high-achieving people with good character if we surround ourselves with them. Consider how much of a negative influence underachievers can have on you. If your five best friends have a negative attitude toward life and are content with subpar performance, there's a good chance that some of that negativity will rub off on you.

The Influence of Others Is Easily Ignored

Aside from aspiring to improve your surroundings, keeping better company can go a long way toward assisting you in reaching your objectives. You can't live your life without people, and the types of people you associate with can have an impact on your work. If your friends, for example, spend a lot of time on their phones and social media, you may be drawn into that cycle of distraction. If you're health-conscious but your coworkers eat cookies and chips all day, it'll be difficult to stick to a nutritious diet. When you're surrounded by people who are focused when they're working, you're more likely to be focused as well. In fact, it's difficult not to be focused because you want to be included and don't want to be the one who breaks someone's concentration. Step into a university library around finals if you've never felt this kind of motivation. Everyone shares a common desire to succeed.


Motivation and dedication are contagious

Consider working on a team where 80 percent of the participants are highly motivated and 20 percent are not. The slackers are in the minority, surrounded by the high achievers. There are only two solutions for the remaining 20%. They can't keep producing mediocre work because the other 80% will not accept it. They will either be persuaded to do better work or will leave because they are unable to keep up. In the end, all of the remaining employees would be highly motivated. The outcome would be different if the percentages of high achievers and unmotivated workers were switched. If 80 percent of workers are unmotivated and 20 percent are highly motivated, the team's output will be of poor quality. High achievers will either lower their own standards or become frustrated with their team members' lax attitude. Finally, all of the remaining team members will deliver uninspired work performance.


You'll accomplish more than you ever imagined possible

When you are surrounded by people who have low standards, you may believe that you do not need to put in extra effort. You may believe you are good enough because you are not comparing your work to that of others who are striving for continuous improvement. This means that, while you may be performing better than the average member of your peer group, you haven't even scratched the surface of your full potential. Highly motivated people are constantly striving for improvement, and spending time with them makes you realize that you, too, have a lot of growing to do. Because you're pushing yourself, you'll make more breakthroughs than you thought possible.


Find Friends Who Aspire to Be the Best

If you're feeling stuck, seek out people with high expectations. Take note of the coworker who is only concerned with producing the best work and the friend who appears to have a clear direction in life. Connect with people who hold themselves and others to high standards. Speak with them to learn how they achieved their level of success. Perhaps they have a philosophy or mindset that you could adopt in order to better yourself. When speaking with these people, try to learn about their perspectives on work, relationships, and life. Examine why they think the way they do. As your relationship grows, you can share your points of view and solicit feedback from them.

As you talk about life and work with them, consider which aspects of their approach you want to incorporate into your own. Try to emulate any mindset or action that has propelled them to success. Positive behavior mimicking can help you change your attitude. This is analogous to forcing yourself to smile in order to feel happy or striking a power pose in order to boost your confidence. This is not the same as blindly imitating what others do in order to be successful. It is necessary to carefully examine the successes of others in order to discover what works best for you.


Every relationship should encourage you to be your best self

It is critical to maintain high standards in all aspects of your life. Look for coworkers, friends, and even a romantic partner who bring out your best qualities. By removing toxicity from your life and surrounding yourself with people who will accept nothing less than excellence from you, you are putting yourself in a position to achieve your goals.
