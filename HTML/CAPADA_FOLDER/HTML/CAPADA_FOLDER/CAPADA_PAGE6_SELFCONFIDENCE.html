Self-Confidence

How to Boost Your Self-Confidence

There are several things you can do to boost your self-esteem. Some of them are minor shifts in your mindset; others will take a little more time to become second nature.


Consider what you've already accomplished

If you believe you haven't accomplished anything, it's easy to lose confidence. Make a list of everything you're proud of in your life, whether it's passing an exam or learning to surf. Keep the list handy and add to it whenever you accomplish something you're proud of. When you're feeling down, pull out the list and remind yourself of all the great things you've accomplished.


Consider what you're good at

Everyone possesses unique skills and abilities. What are yours? Recognizing your strengths and attempting to improve on them will assist you in developing confidence in your own abilities.


Establish some goals

Set some goals and outline the steps necessary to achieve them. They don't have to be lofty; they can be as simple as baking a cake or organizing a night out with friends. Simply set small goals that you can cross off a list to boost your confidence in your ability to complete tasks.


Talk yourself up

You'll never feel confident if you're constantly hearing negative voices in your head telling you that you're no good. Consider your self-talk and how it may be affecting your self-confidence. Treat yourself as you would a best friend, and encourage yourself.


Find a hobby

Find something that you are truly passionate about. Photography, sports, knitting, or anything else! When you've identified your passion, make a commitment to pursuing it. If you are interested or passionate about a particular activity, you are more likely to be motivated and to gain skills more quickly.


Accept Yourself

Nobody is flawless. Everybody has problems and flaws. Accept and love your 'flaws' or imperfections, because they are what make you unique. The key to feeling confident is self-acceptance. When people give you compliments, simply say "thank you," rather than dismissing them or responding with a negative.


If you aren't still feeling better

Sometimes quick fixes don't work in the long run. If you're feeling down and things don't seem to be getting better, it's time to talk to someone who can help. Professionals such as counsellors and psychologists can assist you in developing strategies to boost your confidence. They may also be able to assist you in understanding any underlying issues that are causing you to feel bad about yourself.
