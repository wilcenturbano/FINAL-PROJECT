What exactly are self-conscious feelings?

Self-conscious feelings are those that are influenced by our perceptions of ourselves and how we believe others see us. Emotions such as pride, envy, and humiliation are among them.

Self-awareness and self-consciousness can be positive indicators of emotional maturity. They will assist you in integrating into and functioning within a group. It's common to think that feeling bad after doing something hurtful is a sign of good character. After making a mistake, feeling guilty will help repair relationships. Both positive and negative self-conscious feelings can be balanced and strong motivators.

Excessive self-consciousness can be hazardous to one's health. They have the potential to exacerbate symptoms of anxiety, depression, and borderline personality disorder. They can also make you feel alone and anxious in social situations.

Will self-conscious feelings lead to other problems?

Social anxiety can be caused by an unhealthy sense of self-consciousness. This can lead to feelings of loneliness, which can exacerbate social anxiety. It may also lead to depression and low self-esteem. Traumatic experiences can also be hampered by self-conscious feelings. This is particularly true for patients suffering from mental illnesses. One research looked at women with BPD who had been through a traumatic event. These women were struggling with feelings of humiliation, remorse, and embarrassment as a result of their trauma. These feelings slowed the healing process and exacerbated BPD symptoms.

When people experience such self-conscious feelings, they may avoid seeking medical help. Many people feel shame, guilt, and humiliation while pursuing screening tests for sexually transmitted diseases, according to a report. These feelings were caused by both getting checked and talking to sexual partners. These feelings can cause people to postpone or stop seeking care.
